# World Languages & Culture Guide

A collection of essays exploring the fascinating intersections of language, culture, cognition, and human communication. Each piece dives into a different dimension of how languages shape our world — from the words we borrow across civilizations to the untranslatable emotions only certain cultures have named.

## Essays

| Essay | Topic |
|---|---|
| [Does Your Language Shape How You Think?](essays/linguistic-relativity.md) | The Sapir-Whorf hypothesis, color perception, and how grammar influences thought |
| [The Hidden History Inside Everyday English Words](essays/etymology-everyday-words.md) | Surprising origins of common words from Czech, Japanese, Hindi, Nahuatl, and more |
| [Words That Don't Exist in English](essays/lost-in-translation.md) | Untranslatable concepts like *saudade*, *hygge*, *wabi-sabi*, and *ubuntu* |
| [How Children Learn Languages (And What Adults Can Learn From Them)](essays/how-children-learn-languages.md) | Critical periods, statistical learning, and why immersion works |

## Who This Is For

Whether you're a language learner, a linguistics enthusiast, a teacher looking for discussion material, or simply someone who finds human communication endlessly interesting — these essays are written for you. No jargon required.
